# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RADHA serves Indian retail operators and shoppers who need fast, trustworthy product, expiry, audit, inventory and subscription workflows on mobile-first surfaces. Store owners and staff work in busy retail contexts with low tolerance for slow screens, vague states or fabricated product data. Consumer-facing flows must remain clear enough for quick scans, saved products and product-health decisions.

## Product Purpose

RADHA helps retailers and shoppers identify products, verify EAN and nutrition data, manage expiry risk, run store tasks, handle lightweight inventory and GRN workflows, review reports and manage subscriptions. Success means every surface preserves operational truth: show known data immediately, isolate partial failures, make missing data explicit and never invent health or nutrition values.

## Brand Personality

Warm, exacting, trustworthy. RADHA should feel Indian, premium and practical without becoming decorative or slow. The product voice is calm and direct: it explains what happened, what is still available and what the user can do next.

## Anti-references

RADHA should not look like a generic Material demo, a purple-blue AI SaaS surface, a POS or ERP clone, or a marketing-first app that hides the task behind decoration. Avoid fake health claims, blank error sections, emoji-as-interface, heavy gradients, over-rounded cards, excessive nested cards and stale mobile code overriding backend contract truth.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve known truth before asking for more data.
2. Isolate failures by section so one failed endpoint never blanks a page.
3. Keep free factual product and nutrition information visible while gating premium interpretation.
4. Use RADHA's restrained cream, orange and ink system consistently.
5. Optimize for quick retail action: clear labels, touch-safe controls, retryable states and no avoidable waiting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

RADHA mobile surfaces should maintain at least WCAG AA contrast, meaningful semantics, 44dp or larger touch targets, text-scale resilience through large accessibility sizes and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Localized content must remain complete across English, Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali and Marathi.
